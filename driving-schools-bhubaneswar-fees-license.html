A Quick Note on Fees Before You Read Further

Most private driving schools in Bhubaneswar charge between ‚Çπ3,500 and ‚Çπ6,000 for a 15-day car course. The fee usually includes daily 30-minute sessions, basic traffic rule instruction, and help with your learner's license slot at the Odisha RTO. A permanent driving license (DL) has its own government fees ‚Äî separate from the school's charges ‚Äî paid directly to the RTO.

What I'd Tell a First-Timer in BBSR

  • 1
    Ask specifically about license assistance before you pay. Most schools include it, but "include" can mean anything from "we'll fill your form" to "we'll book your RTO slot and follow up on documents." These are very different levels of service. The answer tells you a lot about the school.
  • 2
    The 15-day course is standard, but 21 days is better if you're a complete beginner. Half an hour per day for 15 days is 7.5 hours behind the wheel ‚Äî workable but thin if you've never driven. Gajanan's 21-day structure gives you 3 more hours of practice, which matters on Bhubaneswar's roads.
  • 3
    Practise in your own car if you own one. Several schools here allow this. The school's training vehicle has dual controls ‚Äî the instructor can brake from the passenger side. Your car doesn't. Learning to trust yourself in your own vehicle before the DL test is worth asking about.
  • 4
    The RTO road test at Nayapalli is straightforward if you've practised the basics. The test route near the Regional Transport Office is not complicated, but first-timers get nervous. Schools that take you on that specific route before test day ‚Äî a few on this list do ‚Äî give you a real advantage.
  • 5
    Don't go by fees alone. The difference between a ‚Çπ3,000 school and a ‚Çπ5,500 school in BBSR often comes down to the instructor you get. Ask around in your neighbourhood or colony. Someone who learned there recently is a better reference than any listing ‚Äî including this one.
