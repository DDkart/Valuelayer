What Is TPCODL and Who Does It Cover?

TPCODL ‚Äî TP Central Odisha Distribution Limited ‚Äî is a joint venture between Tata Power and the Odisha Government that supplies electricity to all of Bhubaneswar, along with Cuttack, Puri, Khurda, Dhenkanal, Nayagarh, and five other districts in central Odisha. If you live anywhere within the Bhubaneswar Municipal Corporation limits ‚Äî Patia, Chandrasekharpur, Old Town, Rasulgarh, Nayapalli, Niladri Vihar ‚Äî your electricity is managed by TPCODL.

The organisation was formerly called CESU (Central Electricity Supply Utility of Odisha) until Tata Power took over distribution operations. Many older residents and even some meter reader slips still say CESU ‚Äî it is the same organisation, now rebranded as TPCODL.

‚ö° Key ID to Save Right Now

Your Consumer Number ‚Äî printed on every electricity bill ‚Äî is the single most important reference for anything you do with TPCODL. Save it in your phone. Every payment, every complaint, every meter-related query requires it.

How to Find Your Consumer Number (If You Lost It)

Your Consumer Number appears on every TPCODL electricity bill. If you have never seen your bill ‚Äî common for tenants in buildings where the landlord pays the bill ‚Äî here is how to find it:

  • 1
    Check the meter box In most Bhubaneswar buildings the consumer number is written in marker or stuck as a label on or near the electricity meter itself. Check the meter box in your flat or on your floor landing.
  • 2
    Ask your landlord For tenants who don't manage the bill directly ‚Äî which is most people in Patia and Chandrasekharpur ‚Äî ask your landlord. It is on the bill they receive and on the connection papers filed during construction.
  • 3
    Call 1912 Give your registered address and mobile number. The operator can retrieve the Consumer Number linked to that address within a few minutes.
  • 4
    Via the Consumer Portal Use the "Forgot Consumer Number" option on portal.tpcentralodisha.com ‚Äî it sends an OTP to the mobile number registered with the account.

What to Do When Your Bill Is Wrong ‚Äî Step by Step

The billing error process has four levels. Knowing all four ‚Äî and the time limits at each level ‚Äî is what separates people who get corrections processed from people who keep paying wrong bills for months.

Level 1: Call 1912 or 1800-345-7122 (Start Here)

The toll-free number 1912 is free from any mobile or landline in Odisha. When you call, the IVR menu matters:

üìû IVR Menu ‚Äî Follow This Exact Order

  1. Press 1 ‚Äî Power supply / voltage issues
  2. Press 2 ‚Äî Safety, fire, emergency
  3. Press 3 ‚Äî Meter reading, billing, payment query ‚Üê your option for billing errors
  4. Press 9 ‚Äî Speak to a customer care agent directly

Always press 3 first ‚Äî it logs your query specifically as a billing issue, routing it to the billing team. When connected, give your Consumer Number, describe the error, and ask for a complaint reference number. Write it down. Without a reference number, you have no proof the complaint was registered.

WhatsApp is genuinely usable ‚Äî send a photo of the disputed bill and they respond during business hours. Email creates a written trail, which is useful if the error is not resolved and you need to escalate.

Level 2: Visit Your Sub-Division Office in Person

If the phone complaint is not resolved within 15 working days, or if the error involves a physical meter issue (burnt meter, wrong reading), visit your TPCODL subdivision office. Bring: your original bill, a copy, your Consumer Number, your Aadhaar, and the reference number from your Level 1 call. Ask to meet the Assistant Engineer (AE) or Sub-Divisional Officer (SDO). Do not just hand papers to the counter staff and leave ‚Äî request an acknowledgement stamp on your application.

Level 3: TPCODL Consumer Grievance Redressal Forum (CGRF)

If Levels 1 and 2 both fail, you have the legal right to escalate to the CGRF ‚Äî the Consumer Grievance Redressal Forum, an independent body mandated by the Electricity Act 2003 and OERC regulations.

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Time Limit

Your CGRF complaint must be filed within 30 days of either: the expiry of the 15-working-day resolution window, or receipt of TPCODL's final response ‚Äî whichever comes first. Missing this window is the most common mistake. Mark your calendar the day you file Level 1.

The Most Common Billing Errors ‚Äî And How to Prove Each One

‚ö° Error 1: Estimated Bill (Average Bill)

When the meter reader cannot access your meter for a month or two ‚Äî locked gate, dog in compound, nobody home during reading time ‚Äî TPCODL generates an "average bill" based on historical consumption. When the actual reading is eventually taken, the difference creates a catch-up bill that looks shockingly high.

How to identify it: The bill will show "E" or "Estimated" in the Current Reading column. What to do: Call 1912, press 3, and request a "special meter reading." TPCODL will send a meter reader at a scheduled time. Any overcharge from the estimated period is adjusted in the next bill.

‚ö° Error 2: Wrong Meter Reading Entered

The meter reader misread a digit ‚Äî ‚Çπ12,500 bill appears when you typically pay ‚Çπ800. More common in Old Town and Rasulgarh where meters are old, digits are worn, and readers are covering large routes quickly.

How to prove it: The monthly photo you took of your meter. If the photo shows 4,230 units and the bill shows a closing reading of 42,300 ‚Äî the reader entered an extra zero ‚Äî your photo wins the argument immediately. What to do: File via 1912 (press 3), attach the meter photo to +91 99378 75999 on WhatsApp, and ask for meter re-reading. TPCODL is required to send a verification team within 7 working days.

‚ö° Error 3: Neighbour's Consumption on Your Meter

Surprisingly common in newly constructed buildings in Kalarahanga, Raghunathpur, and other areas with recent construction, where electrical contractors wire meters incorrectly. Signs: your bill is consistently ‚Çπ200‚Äì‚Çπ400 higher than actual consumption. Verify by switching off your main switch entirely for 10 minutes ‚Äî if your meter still spins, someone else's load is on your meter.

What to do: Film the spinning meter with your main switch off. This video is your evidence. File a complaint in person at the subdivision office. Ask the SDO to send a technical team for "meter audit and connection verification." The process takes 2‚Äì4 weeks but the correction is permanent and you are entitled to a refund.

‚ö° Error 4: Previous Tenant's Arrear on Your New Connection

You move into a flat ‚Äî the previous tenant left without paying three months of bills. TPCODL transfers the arrear to the new consumer at the same address. This is technically incorrect ‚Äî arrears follow the person, not the property, for residential connections. But it happens.

What to do: File a written complaint at the subdivision office with proof that the connection predates your tenancy (your rental agreement start date vs. the arrear period). Request the arrear to be transferred to the previous consumer's name. Ask for the Divisional Manager's counter signature on the acknowledgement.

The One Habit That Solves 80% of Billing Problems

Take a photo of your electricity meter on the 1st of every month. Not a video, not a screenshot ‚Äî a quick dated phone photo of the meter display. It takes 15 seconds. If your bill is ever wrong, this photo is the evidence that resolves it in one call. If you never need it, it costs you nothing.

üì∏ Monthly Meter Photo ‚Äî Who Needs This Most

  1. Patia residents ‚Äî High-density area with large route loads for meter readers. Estimated bills are common in monsoon months.
  2. Raghunathpur & Kalarahanga ‚Äî New construction zones with active wiring errors. Neighbour-on-your-meter cases appear here regularly.
  3. Old Town & Rasulgarh ‚Äî Older meters where digit misreads happen at higher rates than newer meter zones.
  4. Any standalone building ‚Äî Where your meter is in a locked enclosure and the reader may estimate rather than read.
  5. New tenants (0‚Äì6 months) ‚Äî Highest risk period for inheriting previous tenant arrears. Document meter reading on move-in day.
‚úÖ Bottom Line

TPCODL's grievance process works ‚Äî but only if you follow it in sequence, get reference numbers at every step, and meet the 30-day CGRF filing window. The IVR option is Press 3 for billing. The WhatsApp number for billing is +91 99378 75999. And the one habit that prevents most problems: photograph your meter on the 1st of every month.
