The Capital Daily Market ‚Äî locally called the "capital market area" ‚Äî sits adjacent to the main Unit-1 Haat building. The book section is not visible from the road. Walk in past the sabji sellers and follow the inner lane. Capital Book Depot at Shop CV-35 is the anchor here, stocking competitive exam books in Odia, Hindi, and English ‚Äî SSC, Railways, Banking, OPSC, OSSC, and state-level exams.

Several smaller stalls surround it in the same cluster ‚Äî these are the ones worth spending time at. Previous year question papers are almost always available at ‚Çπ30‚Äì80 a piece, compared to ‚Çπ150‚Äì200 in a proper bookshop. The stall owners know the local exam calendar better than most: if OSSC or OPSC prelims are coming up, they stock accordingly.

üí° Unit-1 Insider: If you ask specifically for exam-relevant titles, stall owners often pull things from under the counter that aren't on display. This market is best for SSC, Railways, Banking, and Odia-medium OPSC materials. UPSC range is thinner here ‚Äî go to Master Canteen for that.

Best for: SSC CGL / GD, Railways GK, Banking / IBPS, OSSC state exams, OPSC preliminary booklets in Odia. Not the strongest for full UPSC preparation.

This is the most-recommended second-hand book market in BBSR and has been for at least two decades. There are at least eight permanent second-hand bookshops here, plus a few stalls that appear on busy mornings. One shopkeeper ‚Äî Ranjan, who runs a store with his brother ‚Äî has been in this business since the early 2000s. He buys old books from students who've cleared their exams and resells at 30‚Äì40% below MRP.

This cluster is the right place if you're doing UPSC Civil Services or OPSC OAS preparation. Standard titles are almost always in stock: Laxmikant, Spectrum, Bipin Chandra, India Year Book, Geography by Majid Husain, Economy by Ramesh Singh. You won't find every edition but you'll find most of them. The full NCERT set (Class 6‚Äì12, all subjects) is typically available for ‚Çπ600‚Äì800 ‚Äî versus ‚Çπ2,400+ new.

Padmalaya Medical & General Books on Janpath Road (Plot 31/A, near the Giri Durga Temple junction, Kharvela Nagar) is the best-stocked proper shop in this stretch. The range of a large bookshop, up to 25% discount on new books, and decades of operating history. Open all days including Sunday, 10 AM‚Äì10 PM. If you're new to BBSR and unsure where to start ‚Äî come here first.

A few stalls further down the Janpath toward the Ram Mandir side stock second-hand novels and used guides. The Ram Mandir Vending Zone (Shop No. 66, Janpath) has a dedicated second-hand book dealer who handles competitive titles alongside college-level books.

Best for: Full UPSC and OPSC preparation sets. NCERTs (best selection and lowest prices in the city). Coaching materials from Aakash and Allen (used). Odia literature and general knowledge books.

The Old Book Store in Kharvela Nagar is a permanent shop ‚Äî not a pavement stall ‚Äî and it runs unusually long hours: open till 10 PM every day. That matters significantly if you're working during the day and can only visit in the evening.

Reviewers consistently mention it for competitive exam books specifically: "You can find different books with adequate price ‚Äî materials, textbooks, guidebooks, NCERTs and so on." The selection covers both academic and competitive categories. You can also sell your old books here, though not all coaching-specific titles are accepted ‚Äî call ahead if you're trying to offload specific modules.

The atmosphere is notably library-like ‚Äî quiet and organised for a second-hand store. One student reviewer mentioned spending seven productive hours there simply reading through material before deciding what to buy. That kind of unhurried browsing latitude is uncommon in a commercial bookshop setting.

Best for: Evening and late-night visits. Students who want to browse at length before buying. OSSC and state-level exam books in Odia.

Worth knowing about specifically for UPSC and OPSC Civil Services aspirants. ‚ö†Ô∏è Honest Warning ‚Äî AG Square Pricing

  1. Prices can be marked up without an MRP label. One reviewer flagged that a book quoted at ‚Çπ300 was being sold for ‚Çπ600 without any printed price reference. Come with reference prices already in your head.
  2. The owner can be difficult about exchanges. If you're buying, be certain. Returns are not a standard practice here.
  3. Negotiate firmly. Unlike the friendlier market at Master Canteen, this cluster requires a more assertive approach. If the price seems wrong, say so and be prepared to walk.
  4. The wider complex is useful. Stores around A.N. Book Store in the same Old Bus Stand complex stock Odia-medium competitive guides and GK compilations that are harder to find at Master Canteen.

Best for: UPSC Civil Services aspirants who want Yojana/Kurukshetra back issues, rare UPSC-specific titles, and Odia-medium GK compilations. Go with a price reference and a willingness to negotiate.

These are the most informal spots on this list ‚Äî pavement sellers who spread their stock on tarpaulins or plastic sheets, mostly on weekday mornings and weekend afternoons. The selection rotates constantly because it's built from whatever students sell after their exams. On a good day you can find absolutely current-edition books for 20‚Äì30% of their cover price. On a slow day the stalls might have only outdated syllabus editions.

No fixed address, no phone number, no guaranteed stock. You have to walk past, crouch down, and check what's there. But if you're patient and lucky, the Kalpana Square footpath strip can yield finds that no proper bookshop would offer at that price. It's worth combining with a visit to the Old Book Store at Kharvela Nagar in the same trip ‚Äî both are on the same auto route from the city centre.

Best for: Opportunistic browsing on a patient afternoon. Not reliable for a specific title you urgently need, but potentially the cheapest source in the city for whatever it happens to have on a given day.

Bargaining, Selling, and a Few Honest Notes

  • 1
    You can bargain at almost every stall. Pavement sellers and smaller shops are usually flexible, especially if you're buying multiple books in one go. Buying five or six books? Expect 10‚Äì20% off the quoted price if you ask. Padmalaya and A.N. Book Store are less flexible but not rigid.
  • 2
    You can also sell your old books. Most dealers will buy from you ‚Äî typically 10‚Äì25% of cover price, sometimes higher for in-demand exam-specific titles. If you've finished a batch of OPSC preparation books, selling them before buying the next set helps offset costs significantly.
  • 3
    Watch for outdated editions. UPSC syllabus changed significantly in 2013. OPSC and SSC syllabuses update periodically. Always check the edition and compare it against the current official syllabus notification before paying ‚Äî ask the seller which year the book covers if you're unsure.
  • 4
    Coaching module quality varies. Used Aakash and Allen modules are available at multiple locations. Their value depends entirely on whether the notes and markings inside are clean or chaotic. Always flip through before you pay. Heavily annotated books slow down your reading and can embed someone else's misunderstandings into your study.
  • 5
    The Master Canteen cluster is your best first stop. If you're new to BBSR and don't know where to start, take an auto to "Janpath, Master Canteen side" and walk the lane behind the Tarini Temple. You'll find eight or more shops within a hundred metres and get a feel for the market, the prices, and what's available in one visit.
