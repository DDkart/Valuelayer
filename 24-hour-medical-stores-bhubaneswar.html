What Google Maps Gets Wrong: The Specific Problems

1
Stores that closed but were never removed from Maps At least two stores on the Patia‚ÄìNandankanan Road stretch that show up as "24 hours open" on Google Maps are either permanently closed or changed owners. Always call before driving at night.
2
"24 hours" tags put by owners who meant "we open early and close late" Some owners interpret 24/7 as being available at 5 AM (which they are) and midnight (which they are not). The tag is self-reported and means nothing without verification.
3
Hospital pharmacy listed as the store nearby The most common trap: a medical store on the road outside KIMS or Capital Hospital lists itself with the hospital's name in its title, then adds "24 hours." It is not the hospital pharmacy. The real hospital pharmacy is inside the campus.
4
Old and wrong phone numbers The mobile numbers listed on Google Maps for Bhubaneswar chemist shops are wrong at an alarming rate. Numbers listed on Justdial are more reliable. Always call ahead if it is an unusual hour.
5
Closing times that shift by season Several stores near Saheed Nagar and Ashok Nagar close 30‚Äì45 minutes earlier in winter (November‚ÄìJanuary) because customers taper off. The 11 PM store may close at 10:15 PM in December. This is never reflected on any directory.

The Emergency Protocol: What to Do at 2 AM

If you need medicine in Bhubaneswar at 2 AM and you have no car, here is the fastest sequence to follow:

1

Call the pharmacy inside KIMS Hospital or the Emergency pharmacy at Capital Hospital. Both answer round the clock. Describe what you need and confirm they have it before travelling.

2
Try Medicine Delivery Apps

PharmEasy, Netmeds, or Apollo 247 ‚Äî all have late-night delivery in central Bhubaneswar for common medicines. Delivery takes 30‚Äì60 minutes for urgent orders in Patia and CSP areas.

3

Go directly to the Emergency entrance of KIMS, SUM Hospital, or Capital Hospital. Do not waste time looking for a standalone pharmacy. Hospital pharmacies are part of the emergency system and will assist without a separate OPD ticket.

Several 24/7 petrol stations in Bhubaneswar keep a small supply of basic medicines. The Indian Oil bunk near Patia Chowk and the HP Pump near Saheed Nagar Square sometimes have paracetamol, ORS, and antacids through the night. Not guaranteed, but worth asking.
